What is the difference between a CV and a resume?

In the US, a CV is typically used to apply for academic roles and includes a complete overview of your education and academic experience. Because CVs are comprehensive, they are often several pages long.

A resume, on the other hand, is used for non-academic jobs and focuses on your relevant skills, work experience, and education in a concise one-page format.

Outside of the US, the terms CV and resume are often used interchangeably to describe the document you use to apply for jobs.

Learn more about the differences between CVs and resumes.

What should I include on my CV?

If youre writing a non-academic CV, include these sections:

  • Name and contact information (phone number, email address, and Linkedin profile)
  • Introduction (optional)
  • Education
  • Skills
  • Work history
  • Relevant certifications
  • Other optional sections (languages, awards, volunteer work, etc.)

If youre writing an academic CV, consider adding these additional sections:

  • Teaching experience
  • Publications
  • Conferences and invited talks
  • Professional memberships and associations
  • Research
  • Fellowships
  • Grants and awards

How long should a CV be?

A CV should be a comprehensive summary of your academic and professional achievements such as publications and research, so there is no strict upper limit to its length.

Whats the difference between a CV and a curriculum vitae?

There is no difference CV is simply the abbreviation for curriculum vitae, a Latin term that translates to course of (ones) life.
